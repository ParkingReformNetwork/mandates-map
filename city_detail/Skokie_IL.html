Summary

Multifamily dwellings with 3 or more units and upper story residences as part of mixed-use developments have reduced parking requirements in the B3 Business District, NX Neighborhood Mixed-Use District, TX Transit Mixed-Use District, and CX Core Mixed-Use District. Parking requirements for nonresidential uses in the B6 Downtown Science and Technology district are reduced by 15% from the otherwise required number. Within the B4 Regional Shopping district, parking requirements may be reduced if a transit station serving at least 4 routes exists on-site. Reduced parking requirements apply in the CX Core Mixed-Use District. However, no additional off-street parking is required for existing structures or buildings in the district. No additional off-street parking shall be required for new construction and building additions for consumer goods, food establishments, health and personal care, professional offices, and food services uses when the total gross floor area after the addition is less than 8,000 square feet. Further, the Village Manager may waive an additional 2,000 square feet or the conversion of the existing second floor to a maximum of 2 dwelling units if it can be demonstrated that appropriate shared parking or municipal parking exists in the area. Within a the NX, TX and CX districts, a fee-in-lieu of required parking may be made for nonresidential uses and guest parking for residential uses. A fee-in-lieu of required parking may not be used to reduce residential parking. The Village Manager may waive the contribution for guest parking if it is shown that appropriate shared parking or municipal parking exists in the area.
